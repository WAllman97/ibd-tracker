IBD Tracker — Product Thinking Notes
Core idea

A simple daily tracking app for people with IBD to understand patterns between symptoms, lifestyle, food, stress, sleep, medication and flare-ups.

The app should help users move from vague feelings like “I’ve been bad recently” to clearer insights such as:

“My symptoms tend to worsen after poor sleep, high stress and certain meals.”

1. Who the app is for

The app is for people with Crohn’s disease, ulcerative colitis, IBS-like symptoms, or recurring gut issues who want a simple way to track their condition over time.

Primary user:

Someone managing a chronic gut condition who wants to understand what affects their symptoms without using a complicated medical app.

Secondary users:

People preparing for gastroenterologist appointments
People trying to identify food or lifestyle triggers
People recovering from a flare
People trialling medication, diet changes or supplements
2. Problem it solves

IBD symptoms are hard to remember accurately.

People often forget:

when symptoms started
what they ate
how stressed they were
how well they slept
whether medication was missed
how symptoms changed across weeks or months

This makes it difficult to spot patterns or explain things clearly to doctors.

The app solves this by creating a simple daily log and turning it into useful trends.

3. Main user benefit

The user gets a clearer picture of their gut health over time.

Instead of relying on memory, they can look back and see:

symptom trends
possible food triggers
stress/sleep links
flare warning signs
medication consistency
improvement after lifestyle changes
4. Daily habit

The app should be built around a quick daily check-in.

The user should be able to log the day in under 2 minutes.

Core daily questions:

How was your gut today?
How many bowel movements?
Any urgency?
Any blood?
Pain score?
Energy score?
Stress score?
Sleep hours?
Key foods/meals?
Medication taken?
Notes

The daily habit should feel lightweight, not clinical or annoying.

5. Product promise

Track your symptoms in under 2 minutes a day and discover what may be driving your gut health.

Alternative wording:

A simple IBD tracker that helps you understand symptoms, spot patterns and prepare better for doctor appointments.

6. What makes it different

Most symptom trackers are either too basic, too medical, or too time-consuming.

This app should be:

simple enough to use every day
visual enough to reveal patterns
focused on gut health
useful for both personal insight and doctor conversations
eventually smart enough to suggest possible links between lifestyle factors and symptoms
7. MVP version

The first version should not be overbuilt.

V1 should do three things well:

Let the user enter a daily symptom log.
Save the data.
Show basic trends over time.

That is enough for a useful first product.

8. Future direction

Later versions could include:

food trigger analysis
flare prediction
AI-generated weekly summaries
doctor appointment export
medication tracking
stool chart
wearable/sleep integration
personalised insights
premium subscription features

But these should come after the core tracker works properly.

9. North star

The app should help users answer:

“What is affecting my gut health, and what should I do differently?”

Everything in the app should support that question.
